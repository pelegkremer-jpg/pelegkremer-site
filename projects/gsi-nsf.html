Green Stormwater Infrastructure: Community, Data, AI

NSF SCC-IRG Track 1 integrating community dynamics, environmental data, and AI for sustainable GSI.

Funder

NSF SCC-IRG · Track 1

Period

2024 to 2028

My role

Co-PI

Team

Smith (PI), Jiao, Wadzuk, Kremer, plus Cooney, Bartos, Coonfield, Wolfand, Poor

The question

Green stormwater infrastructure (GSI) is increasingly central to municipal stormwater strategy, but its performance varies widely with site conditions, maintenance, and the social context in which it is embedded. How can cities integrate spatial data with AI and community knowledge to plan, evaluate, and improve GSI in ways that are both effective and equitable?

What this project does

A team of civil engineers, computer engineers, geographers, and community partners working together on a community-integrated AI framework for GSI sustainability. My lab contributes the geospatial analysis and the integration of social and environmental data with the engineering and hydrologic models. The project builds on a prior NSF planning grant (2022–2023) and a CDS&E award on AI for sustainable and fair GSI (2022–2025).
